The company chose EKS over ECS for containerization because they were already heavily invested in Kubernetes for Linux workloads, needed better scalability, required machine access for Windows debugging, and wanted to standardize their orchestration experience, following a minimal-risk approach to stay as close to their existing setup as possible while focusing on application rather than platform changes.
