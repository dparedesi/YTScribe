summary: This keynote presentation introduces AWS Transform, the first agentic AI service for enterprise migration and modernization, launched in May 2025 to address the $2.41 trillion technical debt challenge where 70% of Fortune 500 legacy software is over 20 years old. Dr. Asa Kalavade frames the evolution of migration tools from paper maps and custom scripts to GPS-like intelligent navigation systems, envisioning task agents that understand business context, plan migrations based on objectives, execute transformations, and continuously learn from their trajectories, user input, and enterprise environments. AWS Transform launched with three critical workloads determined by customer priorities: VMware migrations for data center exits requiring assessment of on-prem environments, networking design, wave planning, and execution; mainframe modernization for IBM ZOS workloads driven by AI readiness needs and declining mainframe expertise; and Windows modernization for .NET applications requiring complex dependency understanding. The service addresses the collaborative complexity of transformation projects involving multiple stakeholders, experts, documents, and coordination calls through a purpose-built web application supporting multiple concurrent jobs across workloads, multi-user collaboration with enterprise authentication, a unified artifacts store for document archiving and versioning, and task agents that create and execute job plans through conversational chat interfaces. The architecture relies on three fundamentals: dozens of expert task agents trained on hundreds of thousands of migrations that understand business and enterprise context; goal-based orchestration creating personalized job plans for specific workloads; and continuous learning agents that improve through their own runs, cross-run learning, and next-step recommendations. The presentation details five new VMware task agents including expanded discovery with format deduplication and canonical asset representation, real-time TCO analysis with AWS price catalog integration enabling conversational what-if scenarios completed in minutes rather than weeks, natural language-based migration wave planning considering dependencies and business instructions, enhanced network generation supporting isolated VPCs, hub-and-spoke networks, multiple IP strategies, and multi-account mapping, and integrated Migration Service (MGN) for rehosting operations. A CSL biotech case study demonstrates 10x improvement in application discovery, 12x improvement in migration planning, enabling migration of 17 data centers in 2 years with 30% cost savings. For mainframe modernization, six new agents include natural language analysis of codebases answering questions about batch processes, transactional processes, and application components; activity analysis identifying most complex processes, longest-running operations, and unused components; data analysis for lineage, DB2 tables, and VSAM files; and test agents generating test plans, test datasets, and comparisons. The BMW case study reveals their migration factory approach achieving 75% time savings (10 days to hours for test case creation), 60% increased test coverage, migration of 7 applications in 6 months without additional product team resources, and plans to shorten transformation timelines by 12 months through parallel assembly lines in South Africa and India. The session introduces full stack Windows modernization capabilities enabling simultaneous modernization of application code, SQL Server databases migrating to Aurora PostgreSQL, UI transformation from web forms to Blazor, deployment to EC2 or ECS, and integrated testing, all within a single framework. Transform Custom addresses tech debt management consuming 30% of organizational resources, allowing customers to define custom transformations through code snippets, documentation, and internal wikis, with the system learning and improving transformation definitions through validation, user input, and self-debugging trajectories. The demo shows converting a C++ OpenGL application to Metal, achieving 120 FPS with reduced CPU usage, and campaign management capabilities for scaling transformations across thousands of repositories with CLI integration, pipeline support, and progress tracking. Customer examples include Air Canada modernizing tens of thousands of Lambdas with 90% efficiency and Quad saving over 7,000 hours on ERP modernization. Transform Composability enables partners and consulting companies to create personalized workflows combining their proprietary agents, IP knowledge bases, and enterprise context, with Accenture, Capgemini, and AWS ProServe demonstrating use cases including financial services mainframe transformation, FDIC deposit insurance modernization, and delivery agents reducing time and materials by 30-40%. The vision positions Transform as an IT transformation workbench extending enterprise ecosystems, enabling mix-and-match capabilities across VMware, Windows, mainframe, and custom transformations, with the goal of putting tech debt modernization on autopilot so organizations can invest in innovation rather than maintenance.
