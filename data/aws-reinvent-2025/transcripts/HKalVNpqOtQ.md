The project was driven by critical business pressures: operating under a costly Transitional Service Agreement (TSA) with a fixed end date, needing to migrate over 2 million subscribers from AT&T's legacy IT stack, and the requirement for a modern, scalable architecture to compete with agile rivals. Liberty Latin America, a leading operator across the Caribbean and Latin America with 40 markets and 50,000 km of subsea fiber, faced the dual challenge of lacking a mobile-ready BSS stack (having previously been a fixed-line operator in Puerto Rico) and needing to ensure extreme resiliency in a region frequently battered by hurricanes. The solution centered on a cloud-native architecture anchored by AWS Outposts and Local Zones to achieve the strict 15-millisecond latency requirement for real-time charging (OCS) while ensuring high availability. Flores outlines the architecture: Salesforce manages the product catalog and CRM, Aria handles billing, and Matrix serves as the OCS charging engine. Crucially, the primary Matrix OCS instance runs on an AWS Outpost directly in Puerto Rico to sit next to the mobile core (EPC/5G Core), achieving ~10ms latency. For disaster recovery, a passive OCS instance resides in the AWS Miami Local Zone. Juan Gura then deep-dives into the \"foundation under the sea\"—Liberty's own subsea fiber network (ARCOS and Americas II cables)—which treats the connection to AWS Miami as an extension of their local network, providing the shortest, lowest-jitter path possible. They utilized AWS Direct Connect with dual 100G links for redundancy. The hybrid architecture leverages AWS Transit Gateway as the \"brain\" for routing traffic between the on-premise mobile core, the Outpost in Puerto Rico, the Local Zone in Miami, and the main AWS Region where other SaaS workloads (OSS, mediation) reside. This setup ensures that even if Puerto Rico suffers a catastrophic event like a hurricane, the system can failover to Miami seamlessly. The project not only solved the immediate TSA exit and latency challenges but also established a replicable \"cloud-native telco template\" that Liberty can now deploy across its other markets. Key lessons learned included prioritizing proximity of compute to the network core, choosing agility over heavy customization by adopting SaaS-first principles, and leveraging their own physical network assets to bridge the gap between on-premise constraints and public cloud scalability."
