summary: This lightning talk presented by Mike Levy and Idan Meislitz demonstrates how AWS Marketplace and Amazon Bedrock Agent Core enable customers to rapidly discover, deploy, and operationalize third-party agentic AI solutions by cutting through the hype to make agentic AI tangible and accessible for production workflows. The session defines agentic AI from AWS's perspective as autonomous software systems leveraging AI to reason, plan, and complete tasks on behalf of humans or systems, highlighting their power to think iteratively, create plans, call different tools, and accomplish complex actions through underlying components including large language models functioning as the decision-making brain, various tools, observability, memory, context, and guardrails. Customer adoption is driven by three primary outcomes automating business processes, improving productivity, and reducing costs, with Gartner predicting that by 2028 over thirty-three percent of enterprise software applications will include agentic AI compared to just one percent today, spanning use cases in business workflows like financial reporting and security incident management, workplace productivity integrations with tools like Zoom and Asana, and innovation in fields like engineering, media, life sciences where automation frees human resources for strategic ideation. However, customers face significant challenges transitioning from proof-of-concept to production, with Gartner predicting over forty percent of agentic AI projects may be canceled by 2027 due to lack of business outcomes, increasing costs, scaling difficulties with multi-agent architectures, and unmet business value expectations, prompting AWS to address the fundamental build versus buy dilemma through AI agents and tools in AWS Marketplace launched alongside Amazon Bedrock Agent Core. Amazon Bedrock Agent Core provides modular services comprising essential building blocks for production-ready agents including memory, observability, guardrails, runtime specialized compute resources purpose-built for agents, and newly announced components policy and evaluations in preview, while AWS Marketplace's AI agents and tools category launched in July 2024 with over 900 solutions from several hundred partners now experiencing tremendous growth in vendor-hosted and customer-hosted agents, MCP servers, knowledge bases, SaaS with embedded agents, agent development platforms, and professional services. The integration between Marketplace and Bedrock Agent Core Gateway simplifies third-party tool integration by providing a security layer managing credentials and permissions, enabling use-case-specific gateways that function as tool sets for particular agents, supporting container-based solutions deployed in customer VPCs integrated with runtime, and API-based vendor-hosted solutions accessible through Gateway requiring agents to connect only to one MCP server that provides access to all underlying tools configured within that gateway.
