summary: This session, presented by AWS Solutions Architect Alex Torres and Amazon Stores AI native development leader Steve Tarza, explores how Amazon leverages generative AI to transform developer productivity, addressing the critical statistic that developers spend only 30% of their time writing code while the remaining 70% is consumed by documentation, ticket management, meetings, architectural approvals, security reviews, and operational support. The presentation traces the evolution of AI in software development from 2023, when AI enabled basic code autocompletion, through 2024's production deployments including Rufus, Q Business, Code Whisperer, and Q Developer, to 2025's focus on proven business value and the shift from AI-enhanced tools to AI-native collaborative workflows. Torres outlines the progression from human-needed processes requiring constant oversight, through assistant applications with knowledge bases, to collaborative agentic AI that autonomously handles complex tasks like preparing customer meetings or writing specifications from requirements, and finally to pioneer-stage autonomous agents that spin up, assign tasks, and operate with minimal human governance. The session emphasizes that while AI has dramatically improved the 30% of time spent coding, the greater opportunity lies in automating the 70% of non-coding activities, including specialized agents for writing unit tests, generating documentation, conducting security reviews, and handling code transformations. Torres introduces Strands, AWS's open-source SDK for building repeatable agents that can be developed locally, integrated with MCP servers and custom tools, and deployed using Agent Core for managed runtime with semantic tool discovery, memory management, identity authentication, and comprehensive observability. Tarza then details Amazon's internal journey, beginning in March 2025 when the Storagegen team was tasked with making Amazon developers three times faster at delivering features to customers, working within Amazon's two-pizza team culture where thousands of small teams have full end-to-end ownership, face constraints on time and resources, and struggle with operational support, technical debt, and maintaining consistency across thousands of teams. The team adopted startup practices of rapid experimentation, failing fast, and scaling successful solutions, progressing through experiment, scale, mature, and impact phases, with aggressive timelines that accelerated due to significant demand for their products. Tarza defines AI-native solutions as having three attributes: being proactive and independently driving activity without human prompting, being intent-driven by translating goals into coordinated actions across tools and agents, and being deeply contextual by reasoning over persistent organizational knowledge and understanding team history and business objectives. The first solution presented is spec-driven development, which shifts from writing code to writing specifications that AI can automatically generate code from, providing an 80% first draft that developers can refine, while specifications also serve as context for documentation, tests, and validations, creating a unified source of truth for stakeholders and enabling consistent cross-platform implementations and technical debt elimination through re-implementation. Spec Studio, Amazon's internal tool, automatically generates specifications from existing code through code-to-spec functionality, extracting system boundaries, capabilities, service dependencies, and detailed requirements even from uncommented code, with citations linking specifications back to source code lines, enabling semantic search across all package specifications and deep Q&A capabilities, while allowing human feedback to correct AI-generated specifications that can be incorporated into future generations. The second solution, AI Teammate, represents a paradigm shift from one-on-one AI interactions to AI as a proactive team member that joins teams, connects to all team systems like Slack and ticketing systems, continuously learns from team activities, suggests and takes actions throughout the development lifecycle, handles routine tasks automatically including answering questions, drafting documents, and executing tool-backed actions, and creates persistent team memory that maintains context across systems and communications, allowing developers to focus on high-judgment activities like architecture and design decisions. Tarza demonstrates AI Teammate's capabilities through examples showing it querying Spec Studio to answer questions about packages, combining specification knowledge with team dialogue history, generating tasks from feature request documents, proactively creating tasks in project management systems, generating first-draft implementations while teams sleep, conducting code reviews with automatic email notifications, and iterating on code based on developer feedback in a fully automated workflow. The architecture uses event sources from developer systems feeding into Lambda connectors pushing to streams, with batching to process information rationally, memory capture that compresses disparate events into context windows, proactive decision-making by the AI about whether to take action, tool invocation including modeling Slack messaging as a tool, and agent-to-agent connections that scale expertise through reusable agent capabilities, coordinate complex tasks autonomously, reduce human handoffs, and maintain consistent context from planning through execution to documentation. Results achieved by November 2025 include four times the number of features shipped to customers for subset teams, reduction in operations and routine tasks, improved quality, and implementations compressed from weeks to hours, with key learnings emphasizing the shift from focusing on efficiency to reimagining processes entirely, staying flexible in approach as plans evolved, and recognizing that AI-native teams represent collaboration between AI and humans working together in parallel. The session concludes with plans to scale AI-native solutions to thousands of teams with a 2026 goal of 75% of stores teams using these solutions, integration with Quiro for accelerating code development, and takeaways emphasizing that software development is evolving rapidly, the importance of standardization and consistency across teams, building persistent memory that survives developer turnover, and evaluating new capabilities to push the frontier forward while ensuring consistent results that enable teams to deliver faster and dream bigger.
