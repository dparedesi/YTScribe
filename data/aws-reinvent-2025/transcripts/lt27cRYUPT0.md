summary: This session introduces AWS Billing Transfer, a new service now generally available through AWS Billing and Cost Management that transforms channel partner billing and governance, addressing fundamental conflicts in the traditional AWS account construct where management accounts control both administrative functions and billing. The traditional model created significant challenges: management accounts have full administrative control including service control policies, multi-account governance settings like GuardDuty, Control Tower, and landing zones, while also being responsible for organization-wide billing, forcing channel partners to control management accounts for billing access while end customers operated in member accounts, resulting in conflicts over administrative access control where partners and customers had to compromise. This created three major problems: security and autonomy conflicts requiring end customers to delegate governance controls creating security risks, operational inefficiencies forcing partners to create dozens or hundreds of organizations and retrieve billing artifacts monthly from each to manage multi-account reselling businesses, and scalability challenges making deals harder to close because partners had to convince customers why they needed management account control while managing multiple customer bases across separate accounts. Billing Transfer introduces three key benefits addressing these challenges: enhanced security and autonomy allowing end customers to operate in entirely separate organizations from resellers so both entities self-control their own AWS organizations, simplified billing operations enabling partners to consolidate billing in a single organization across all end customers instead of proliferating organizations, and proprietary discount protection delivering bills and chargeable artifacts exclusively to partners in their own organizations while customers maintain self-governance in separate organizations. The technical implementation allows management accounts of one organization to delegate billing to a separate AWS organization, enabling partners and end customers to operate separately with partners exclusively managing billing and customers retaining full organizational control. For partners, centralized management allows managing multiple end customers from a single partner account receiving bills, margin privacy without requiring customers to sacrifice management account control as chargeable artifacts deliver exclusively to partners, and operational efficiency eliminating the need to manage hundreds of customer accounts. For end customers, complete environment control eliminates trade-offs when onboarding to resellers, first-time cost visibility through native AWS cost management tooling including Cost Explorer, cost and usage reports, and soon cost anomaly detection and RISP reports within their own separate organizations while procuring through channel partners, making sales conversations easier by creating an even playing field for partners to compete delivering services. The billing experience separates partner and customer organizations: customers receive no AWS invoices or bills but view costs at rates determined by solution providers through AWS Billing Conductor showing custom rates or public pricing, while partners receive chargeable AWS invoices with actual prices paid and can toggle between chargeable costs and pro forma costs (the customer's Cost Explorer experience) in their management account. Centralized billing delivery allows a single management account to receive invoices across multiple customers, maintaining bill segregation for existing third-party billing tool integrations while customers maintain separate organizations. The new AWS Partner Central experience enables easier qualification for channel benefits and discounts on customer consumption, centralized account management against channel programs with self-activation eliminating manual approvals, and establishing customer relationships once per organization at the management account level inherited by all member accounts, ending per-account end user reporting. Public APIs enable integration with CRM or first-party tooling for channel ecosystem reporting, with new Billing and Cost Management APIs automating billing operations and customer onboarding through billing transfer. Key transformational benefits include growing faster by offering end customers control without procurement channel trade-offs, leaving root access to customers who retain organizational control and self-governance while partners manage billing and deliver value-added services, simplifying pre-sales and sales conversations to close deals easier. Billing and sales operations simplify with all bills in a single AWS account, native rerating through Billing Conductor, automated channel program reporting eliminating manual processes for channel discounts, enabling automation of FinOps workflows and reducing third-party tooling stack complexity. Together these benefits help partners scale more profitably with simplified customer workflows for faster growth and reduced operational costs per customer account. Launch partners who provided extensive beta feedback include SoftwareOne, SHI, TD SYNNEX, Reddington, and Commit. The service is officially generally available through AWS Partner Central (accessible through AWS Console), with technical guides helping partners add new customers to billing transfer and migrate existing customers with root access conflicts so they can reclaim organizational control.
