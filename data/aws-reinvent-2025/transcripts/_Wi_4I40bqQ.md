  This AWS re:Invent 2025 session explores the agentic future of financial services, presenting a vision where autonomous AI systems transform how financial institutions operate and how consumers interact with financial products. Scott Mullins, Managing Director of Financial Services at AWS, opens by highlighting how financial institutions have been preparing for this moment by moving data to AWS and establishing secure guardrails for generative AI adoption. The session features two customer presentations: Axel Shell from Allianz Technology demonstrates how the insurance giant is building a framework for deploying AI-powered solutions at scale, showcasing agentic browsing capabilities that allow purchasing pet insurance through a single prompt in 4-5 minutes, and revealing Allianz's multi-agent architecture with utility agents, business agents, and planner agents organized in a hierarchical Gen AI mesh to avoid the complexity pitfalls of siloed POCs. The company has implemented a claims processing system using seven specialized agents (planner, cyber, coverage, weather, fraud, payout, and audit agents) that reduced processing time by 80% from 100 days, with a real-world example of handling a power outage claim where agents automatically verify coverage, check weather incidents, detect fraud, and process payouts while maintaining full audit trails for regulatory compliance. Eric Rappel from Coinbase Developer Platform then presents X402, an open standard for internet-native payments designed to enable agent-to-agent commerce using stablecoins, addressing the fundamental problem that the internet was never designed with native payment capabilities despite the HTTP 402 status code being reserved for this purpose. X402 leverages stablecoins like USDC to enable 24/7 availability, instant settlement in seconds rather than days, and incredibly low transaction fees of approximately 0.1 cents regardless of transaction size, with the protocol already processing over 70 million transactions and $38 million in volume in the last 30 days. The standard enables dynamic agents that can discover and pay for services on-demand without pre-existing integrations, creating isolated fund silos with spending rules and enabling micropayments that make paying 5 cents for data access economically viable, while solving the critical problem that AI agents scraping content bypass traditional advertising monetization models, allowing direct payment for content access within the same request. Both presentations emphasize that building the agentic future requires trust, new standards, and systematic approaches to governance, with Allianz focusing on creating reusable agent marketplaces and orchestration platforms to avoid complexity scaling issues, while Coinbase advocates for open standards to prevent walled gardens and maintain internet openness in the agentic age.
